Lesson 16: Urban Ecology

Designing "Sponge Cities" Where Nature and Skyscrapers Coexist

Urban ecology integrates natural systems into cities to improve sustainability. "Sponge cities" absorb and manage stormwater like natural sponges, reducing flooding and pollution. Green roofs, vertical gardens, and permeable pavements enhance biodiversity and air quality.

These designs create resilient urban environments that support wildlife, reduce heat islands, and improve human health. Urban ecology balances development with conservation.

Academic Discussion: Urban Challenges

Teacher: "How do cities impact ecosystems?"
Student: "They create heat islands, reduce green space, and increase runoff."
Teacher: "Yes, but urban ecology mitigates this. Sponge cities manage water better."
Student: "What are green roofs?"
Teacher: "Vegetated rooftops that insulate buildings and support biodiversity."
Student: "What is urban ecology?"
Teacher: "Integrating natural systems into cities for sustainability. Includes green spaces, wildlife corridors, and water management."
Student: "Sponge cities?"
Teacher: "Cities designed to absorb rainwater, reduce flooding, and recharge groundwater using permeable surfaces and green infrastructure."
Student: "Green roofs?"
Teacher: "Vegetated rooftops that insulate buildings, reduce heat islands, and provide habitats for birds and insects."
Student: "Benefits?"
Teacher: "Improved air quality, cooler temperatures, biodiversity support, and enhanced mental health."
Student: "Challenges?"
Teacher: "Space constraints, maintenance costs, and policy support. But benefits outweigh costs."
Student: "Examples?"
Teacher: "Singapore's vertical gardens, Copenhagen's bike infrastructure, and New York's High Line park."

Material: Implementing Urban Ecology

Cities like Singapore and Copenhagen lead in green infrastructure. Policies encourage green buildings and wildlife corridors. Benefits include cooler temperatures, cleaner air, and enhanced well-being.

Community involvement is key to successful urban ecological design.

Vocabulary Focus

Sponge CityUrban area designed to absorb and reuse stormwater.
Heat IslandUrban area significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas.
Green RoofRoof covered with vegetation for environmental benefits.
Permeable PavementPavement that allows water to infiltrate the ground.
